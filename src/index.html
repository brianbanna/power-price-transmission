A data story about European electricity markets

The Price of Wind and Sun

Switzerland produces almost no solar power. Yet on a spring Sunday in 2024, its electricity price dropped lower than Germany's. This is how one country's renewable revolution is rewiring the economics of its neighbours.

Step 1 · 2024-05-12 · 00:00 CET

A Sunday in spring.

At midnight, electricity costs roughly the same across central Europe. Around forty-five euros per megawatt-hour. The grid is calm.

Step 2 · 06:00 → 12:00

Then the sun rises.

Germany has forty-two gigawatts of solar capacity. On a clear spring Sunday, there is more electricity than anyone needs.

Step 3 · 13:00 CET

Negative.

Switzerland produces almost no solar power. But on this Sunday, its electricity price dropped lower than Germany's. The grid doesn't respect borders.
